Eating and Drinking

AU CAFÉ

In the café

   

In a French café, one can choose the cheaper option of being seated at the counter. The second option is being served by a waiter at the table. Tipping is normal in French dining, should the food and service be pleasing to the customer. In the mornings, it is likely that you will be served bread and croissants more than any other form of breakfast.

Cultural tip A coffee in France is normally served small and black. For any other variety of coffee, this will have to be requested, and you can do so by asking for milk (lait froid/ lay frwah, which translates as cold milk) If this is not specified by you, you may find yourself being given a jug of hot milk!

AU RESTAURANT

In the restaurant

France is brimming with places to eat. For example, a café holds authentic French snacks that you can enjoy. There is also the brasserie, which refers to a traditional French restaurant. Booking is not necessary to be able to dine in a brasserie, and the service is famously fast! On the other hand, gastronomic restaurants are the places that require booking and smart wear from the customers (be warned if you ever wish to eat in a French gastronomic restaurant!)

Memorise these words, which can be useful in a restaurant.

la carte (lah kart) menu
les entrées (f) (lay zontray) starters
les plats (m) (lay plah) main courses
les desserts (m) (lay dessair) desserts
le déjeuner (luh dayjunay) lunch
le dîner (luh deenay) dinner
le petit-déjeuner (luh puhtee dayjunay) breakfast

VOULOIR

To want

Vouloir (to want) is an essential verb in everyday French conversation.

Below is a table showing the different forms of vouloir (to want) Try to say them out loud.

Phonetic spelling is provided below each French word.

je veux (juh vuh) I want
tu veux (tew vuh) you want (informal)
il/ elle veut (eel/ el vuh) he/ she wants
nous voulons (noo vooloñ) we want
vous voulez (voo voolay) you want (formal/ plural)
ils/ elles veulent (eel/ el verl) they want

LES PLATS

Dishes

Fine dining is what makes France very famous indeed. She offers a wide variety of regional dishes! Typically, a lot of garlic and butter are involved in a fine French dish. In history, French cusine mostly comprised, but we can see with the emergence of more and more vegetarian options in French gastronomy.

Cultural tip In a typical French restaurant, you may be given two options to choose from: eating a set menu or ordering à la carte With a set menu, the starter is normally a salad, and you are given the choice between cheese or dessert. À la carte, contrastingly, refers to ordering food as separate entities, as and when you desire the specific meal.
